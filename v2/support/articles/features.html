Unlike standard word processors, CiteCount provides multiple counting methods tailored for academic standards:

Words without Citations

üìÑ

üéØ Automatic Citation Detection

How It Works

CiteCount uses intelligent pattern recognition to automatically identify in-text citations in your document. Detected citations are highlighted in red (included in word count) or green (excluded from word count).

Supported Citation Formats

Format Example Detection
Author-Date (Smith, 2020) ‚úÖ Automatic
Multiple Authors (Smith & Jones, 2020) ‚úÖ Automatic
Et al. (Smith et al., 2020) ‚úÖ Automatic
Page Numbers (Smith, 2020, p. 45) ‚úÖ Automatic
Multiple Citations (Smith, 2020; Jones, 2019) ‚úÖ Automatic
Numeric [1], [2,3], [4-6] ‚úÖ Automatic

Citation Detection Features

  • Real-time Detection: Citations are detected as you type
  • Visual Highlighting: Red (counted) or green (excluded) highlighting
  • Click to Jump: Click any citation in the panel to jump to its location
  • Toggle Inclusion: Click citations to include/exclude from count
  • Citation List: View all citations in the Citations panel
  • Word Count per Citation: See how many words each citation contains
  • Occurrence Count: See how many times each citation appears
‚ö†Ô∏è Note: Citation detection works best with standard formatting. Unusual spacing, special characters, or non-standard formats may not be detected. You can manually toggle citations in the Citations panel if needed.

üîó Citation Generator

Quick Citation Creation

Generate properly formatted citations without leaving CiteCount:

Access the Generator

  • Click the Citation Generator button in the toolbar
  • Or press Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + C

Supported Citation Styles

  • APA (7th Edition): Most common for social sciences
  • MLA (9th Edition): Humanities and liberal arts
  • Chicago: Author-date and notes styles
  • Harvard: UK universities

Source Types

  • Books
  • Journal Articles
  • Websites
  • Newspapers
  • And more...

How to Use

  1. Click Citation Generator or press Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + C
  2. Select citation style (APA, MLA, etc.)
  3. Choose source type (Book, Article, Website)
  4. Fill in the required fields (author, title, year, etc.)
  5. Click "Generate" to create the citation
  6. Copy to clipboard or insert directly into your document
‚ú® Pro Tip: The citation generator creates both in-text citations and full references for your bibliography!

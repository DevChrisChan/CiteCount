Welcome to CiteCount! CiteCount is a tool designed to help you count words in your text while excluding in-text citations.

CiteCount automatically detects your in-text citation and highlights it (CiteCount, 2026).

By default, all citations are not included in the Total Words count. They are visualized by a red highlight in the text. If CiteCount misrecognized an in-text citation (since CiteCount detects citations in parenthesis, like this one), you can always toggle it to count into the total word count.

You can click on a citation and CiteCount will jump to its position ("What's new in CiteCount v2", 2026).

Core Counting Engine

Most accurate word-counting algorithms

CiteCount understands every available word count matters, and we deliver the most accurate, citation-aware counting experience. Our advanced algorithms gives you the complete picture: clean counts, citation-separated totals, and time estimates for reading, speaking, and handwriting.

Primary metric

Words Without Citations

The count your examiner actually grades. All in-text citations and reference lists are detected and excluded automatically.

Secondary metric

Total Word Count

The full count including all citations, shown alongside the clean count so you always know both figures at once.

Detail

Character Counting

Separate character counts with and without citations, essential for assignments that specify character limits.

Detection

Citation Highlighting

Every detected citation is highlighted red in your text. The sidebar shows words, occurrence count, and lets you exclude individual ones.

Welcome to CiteCount

Word counting solution for students and researchers, built by an IB student.

What is CiteCount?

CiteCount is a tool to count words without citations from your text. Do you have an assignment which requires you to include a word count without citations? Supporting MLA, APA and Chicago style, CiteCount is the tool for you.

With CiteCount, you can track and count number of citations you have used, words you have used without citations, and characters you have used without citations.

We provide an advanced algorithms to ensure you get the most accurate citation counts - all done within your browser.

AutoSave is integrated to the text editor of CiteCount, making sure you won't lose any changes while editing, even if you leave the site and come back later.

Word counts are real time updated, so you can see the results as you type.

CiteCount supports languages from all over the world, and strives to provide 100% accurate word counting tools. CiteCount is proud to be more accurate than word-processing software such as Microsoft Word and Google Docs*. Our features expand as users use CiteCount. Feel free to submit feedback at cchc [at] duck [dot] com

Most importantly, this web application is ad free and open source. You can use it for free, without any restrictions. View the source on GitHub.

* Microsoft Word treats punctuation marks in Chinese as a word, however it shouldn't be included. CiteCount addresses this problem.
* Google Docs reports incorrect word counts with languages such as Chinese. It also incorrectly reports links as multiple words in English. CiteCount addresses this problem.

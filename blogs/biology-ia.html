Crafting Your IB Biology IA

Introduction

If you're currently working on your IB Biology Internal Assessment (IA) and feeling uncertain about the requirements, you’re not alone! This guide is designed to help you navigate the essential word count and structure of your report, ultimately boosting your chances of success.

Your Biology IA should be a comprehensive investigation that spans up to 3,000 words. While there is no strict page limit, it's advisable to use a 12-point font with double spacing for clarity and readability.

Understanding the Structure of Your IA

The basic structure of your IA includes three main sections: the introduction, the main body, and the conclusion. Each section plays a critical role in presenting your research effectively.

Introduction (Approximately 500 words)

Begin your report by clearly stating your research question and providing context for your study. Justify your hypothesis based on theoretical knowledge and demonstrate a solid understanding of relevant biological concepts. Use specific terminology appropriately and show personal engagement with the topic. It's important to highlight your initiative throughout the research process and how it relates to your interests.

Main Body (Approximately 2,000 words)

This section is where you detail your investigation and analysis. Start with a clear methodology that outlines the materials and equipment used in your experiment, followed by step-by-step procedures. Remember to address measurement uncertainties and differentiate between controlled and uncontrolled variables, discussing their potential impact on your results.

Once you've outlined your methodology, present your results clearly. Analyze the data trends, investigate anomalies, and include relevant quantitative and qualitative data to support your conclusions. Utilize graphs, tables, and images for clarity in data presentation.

Conclusion (Approximately 500 words)

Your conclusion should directly answer the research question while evaluating the investigation's strengths and weaknesses. Discuss any environmental concerns related to resource use during your experiment and suggest improvements for future investigations. Finally, propose potential areas for further research.

Important Reminder!

Remember to exclude the following from your word count:

  • Acknowledgments
  • Contents page
  • Diagrams
  • Labels of five words or fewer
  • Headings on diagrams of ten words or fewer
  • Tables of statistical data
  • Equations, formulas, and calculations
  • Citations (must be included in the body of the essay)
  • References (to be included in footnotes/endnotes)
  • Bibliography
  • Appendices

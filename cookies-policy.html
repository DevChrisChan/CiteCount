How CiteCount uses cookies and similar technologies

Last updated: August 9, 2025 Applies to: citecount.com

Quick Summary: CiteCount uses minimal cookies to provide essential functionality and optional analytics. You have full control over non-essential cookies through our cookie settings.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better experience by remembering your preferences and understanding how you use our site.

Similar technologies include:

  • Local Storage: Browser-based storage for your preferences and saved content.
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage that expires when you close your browser.
  • Web Beacons: Small transparent images used for analytics.

2. How We Use Cookies

CiteCount uses cookies for the following purposes:

Essential Functionality

  • Saving your text and preferences locally.
  • Remembering your settings and customizations.
  • Ensuring the application works properly.
  • Security and fraud prevention.

Analytics and Performance

  • Understanding how users interact with our features.
  • Identifying areas for improvement.
  • Measuring the effectiveness of new features.
  • Analyzing usage patterns (anonymously).

User Experience

  • Remembering your theme preference (light/dark mode).
  • Storing your cookie consent choices.
  • Maintaining your session across page reloads.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration Required
Strictly Necessary Essential for basic functionality, security, and AutoSave features. Persistent (until you clear them). Yes
Analytics Help us understand usage patterns and improve the service. Up to 2 years. No (requires consent)
Performance Monitor site performance and identify technical issues. Session or up to 1 year. No (requires consent)

5. Your Cookie Choices

You have several options for controlling cookies on CiteCount:

Cookie Settings (Recommended)

Use our cookie preference center to choose which types of cookies you want to allow:

Browser Settings

You can also control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Block all cookies: Your browser can be set to block all cookies.
  • Delete cookies: You can delete existing cookies from your browser.
  • Selective blocking: Most browsers allow you to block third-party cookies while allowing first-party cookies.

Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect your experience:

  • Essential cookies: Disabling these will prevent core functionality like AutoSave.
  • Analytics cookies: Disabling these won't affect functionality but helps us improve the service.
  • Preference cookies: Disabling these means your settings won't be remembered.

6. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our site are set by third-party services. We carefully select our partners to ensure they respect your privacy:

  • We only work with privacy-respecting analytics providers.
  • Third-party cookies are subject to their own privacy policies.
  • You can opt out of third-party cookies through our cookie settings.
  • We regularly review our third-party integrations.

7. Cookie Security

We implement security measures to protect cookie data:

  • Secure transmission: Cookies are transmitted over HTTPS.
  • Limited scope: Cookies are restricted to necessary domains only.
  • No sensitive data: We never store sensitive personal information in cookies.
  • Regular audits: We regularly review our cookie usage.

8. International Transfers

Some of our third-party services may transfer cookie data internationally:

  • Google Analytics may transfer data to the United States.
  • All transfers comply with applicable data protection laws.
  • Appropriate safeguards are in place for international transfers.
  • Data is always processed in accordance with our privacy commitments.
